Current Threat Alert Log

Impersonation Phishing

Beware of emails/texts demanding immediate action or credential verification from banks or authorities. Action: Always verify using official contact numbers, never click links in suspicious messages.

High-Yield Investment Fraud

Scams promising unrealistically high returns with zero risk, often using sophisticated websites or deepfake videos. Action: Consult a licensed financial advisor before investing. If it sounds too good, it is a risk.

Unsolicited Remote Access

Unwanted calls claiming a virus is on your PC to trick you into granting remote control. Action: Never grant remote access to unsolicited callers. Hang up and contact your provider directly.

Financial Transaction Laundering

Employment offers or requests to use your personal bank account to transfer funds for a fee. Action: This is illegal money laundering. Refuse immediately to avoid criminal liability.

Incident Response Flow

If funds are compromised, follow these critical steps immediately for optimal recovery:

1. Call 1930

The first and most critical step. Contact the National Cyber Crime Helpline (1930) to initiate an immediate fund freeze across banking channels.

2. Formalize Report

Submit a detailed report on the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal. Gather all evidence (logs, screenshots) before starting the online process.
